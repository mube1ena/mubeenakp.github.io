Instagram Reels: Reducing Cognitive Fatigue

Product Improvement Proposal | User Research & Feature Prioritization
User Research Feature Prioritization Metrics Analysis Product Strategy

üéØ Problem Statement

Instagram Reels drives high engagement, but users experience mental fatigue and screen-time guilt, leading to cyclical uninstall/reinstall behavior. How might we reduce cognitive fatigue while protecting ad revenue?

üîç Research Methodology

Conducted quantitative survey with 29 participants (ages 18-24, CS graduates, daily Reels users in India)

  • Survey Design: 15 questions covering usage patterns, pain points, and feature preferences
  • Data Analysis: Identified patterns in user behavior, churn risk, and satisfaction metrics
  • User Segmentation: Categorized users by usage intensity and churn risk

üìä Key Insights

44.8% Uninstalled due to fatigue
83.3% Use Reels daily (multiple sessions)
34.5% Uninstalled multiple times

Core Insight: High engagement correlates with high churn risk. Users love Reels but feel guilty about time spent, leading to cyclical uninstall behavior.

üí° Proposed Solutions

  • Mood-Based Content Filters: Allow users to select mood/energy level (Fun, Chill, Learning) to personalize feed and reduce dopamine overdrive
  • Smart Pause Cards: AI-driven break prompts after extended scrolling sessions, reducing cognitive fatigue
  • Time Limit Controls: Gentle reminders and customizable break intervals to help users maintain self-control

üéØ Feature Prioritization

Used user interest survey data + business impact analysis:

  • #1 Priority - Mood Filters: 72.4% user interest, addresses content quality (highest user demand)
  • #2 Priority - Time Limits: 41.4% rated as "most helpful," critical behavioral tool
  • #3 Priority - Pause Cards: Low user preference (10.3%) but mandatory AI-driven intervention to protect high-risk users

üìà Expected Impact

Success Metrics:
  • Reduction in uninstall rate due to fatigue (target: 20% decrease)
  • Increase in engagement time with higher satisfaction scores
  • Higher Reels saves & shares (indicator of content quality perception)
  • Maintain or increase ad impressions (protect revenue)

üèÜ Outcome

This case study demonstrated user research, data analysis, and product thinking skills. Received Product Manager offer from SarvmAI based on this work, validating the approach and prioritization framework.

üéØ Challenge

Kerala Startup Mission needed to scale their Women in Deep Tech initiative to reach more women entrepreneurs across Kerala. The existing program lacked structured onboarding and engagement frameworks.

üë§ My Role

Program Manager & Community Product Designer

  • Designed community structure and onboarding experience from scratch

  • Built feedback collection systems and iteration cycles

üîç User Research Process

  • Surveys & Interviews: Conducted feedback sessions with 50+ community members

üí° Solution Design

Engagement Framework:

  • Weekly touchpoints (events, workshops, networking sessions)
  • Quarterly feedback surveys with action items
  • Recognition system for active members
  • Clear progression path (member ‚Üí contributor ‚Üí leader)

92% Satisfaction Score
40% YoY Growth
20% Increase in participation

üìö Scalable Framework Created

Documented learnings into a Community Engagement Playbook now being implemented across 50+ colleges in Kerala. The playbook includes:

  • Onboarding templates and checklists
  • Event planning frameworks
  • Feedback collection systems
  • Community health metrics dashboard
  • Best practices for stakeholder management

üéì Key Learnings

  • User research is critical: Early feedback loops prevented building wrong features
